Welcome!

I'm Caleb Charpentier, a Ph.D. student in Uyeda Lab at Virginia Tech. I'm interested in the evolution of morphology, and how we can incorporate Knowledge-Guided Machine Learning (KGML) and automated discovery techniques to rethink the ways that we study complex traits across time scales.

I'm currently in my second year of my program, and am mostly focused on developing a pipeline to simulate raw data, such as images of imaginary taxa, that can be used to assess the performance of neural networks that are intended to discover traits. The pipeline is called TraitBlender, and can be found here.

In the past, the way we constructed characters was often limited by expert knowledge / interest, which made some research difficult to reproduce and limited us to measuring things that a human is likely to notice. Deep learning can help us cross these barriers, by making character construction more objective and allowing us to measure exceedingly complex and high-dimensional features.
